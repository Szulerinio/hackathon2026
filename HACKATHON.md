## The Assignment

Build a personal CRM for a specific person. Your first job as a team is to pick that person from the persona list (first come, first served — claim yours on the shared board in the first 30 minutes). The judges will ask who you built this for, and **"everyone" is a wrong answer.**

### What a personal CRM is, for our purpose

A place where someone stores the people in their life and the context around them — who they are, how you know them, what you last talked about, what matters to them, what you owe each other — and which **actively helps them be better at those relationships**. The "actively helps" part is where the interesting work lives.

---

## Your Persona: Rafał

**Age:** 38  
**Role:** Real estate agent in a mid-size city

**His network:**
Past clients (who refer), current clients (who buy), contractors, mortgage brokers, lawyers. A house sale isn't a transaction, it's a five-year relationship, and he has no system for "check in with everyone I sold to three years ago."

> **Note:** For Rafał, a contact often represents a household or couple rather than a single individual — how to model that is a product decision the team should make.

---

## Your CRM Should Be Able to Answer Questions Like These

*(Not literally these — use them as a compass for what "good" looks like.)*

- Who should I reach out to this week, and why?
- Who do I know that could introduce me to someone working on X?
- What did I last talk about with Anna, and what should I follow up on?
- I just met someone named Marcin at a conference — do I already know him? Do we have people in common?
- I'm traveling to Berlin next week. Who should I try to see?
- Remind me what's going on in Piotr's life right now.

If your CRM can answer three of these convincingly for your chosen persona, you're in good shape. If it can answer one of them in a way that makes a judge go *"oh, damn"* — you're probably winning.

---

## Rules

- Use any tools you want: Cursor, Claude Code, Lovable, Bolt, Figma Make, v0, Claude Projects, Notion, Airtable, Zapier, Supabase — whatever gets you there. Existing products as the backbone is fine and encouraged.
- At least one working end-to-end user flow must run live in the demo. **Live beats recorded; recorded beats broken.**
- Seed your system with **30–100 realistic fake contacts**. A CRM with three contacts in it demos badly.
- **One feature done well beats six half-features.**

---

## What We Are NOT Grading On

- **Technical sophistication for its own sake.** A team that wires Claude to a Google Sheet with a clever prompt can beat a team that built a custom RAG pipeline, if the Sheet team tells a better story about a better product.
- **Code quality.** This is a hackathon.
- **Feature count.**
